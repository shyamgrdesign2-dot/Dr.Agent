Domain-Specific	Principles	—	From	Our	Research
PRINCIPLE	01
Glanceable,	Not	Scrollable
68%	of	dictations	are	150–400	chars.	Doctors	spend	<2	seconds	per	sidebar	glance.	Every	summary	≤	3	lines.	Every
alert	≤	1	line.	If	scrolling	is	required,	the	design	failed.
PRINCIPLE	02
Mirror	the	Mental	Model,	Not	the	Textbook
Medical	textbooks:	History	→	Exam	→	Dx	→	Rx.	Real	doctors:	Meds	(46%)	→	Symptoms	(21%)	→	Vitals	(15%)	→	History
(5%).	We	design	for	reality.	Priority	=	frequency	×	clinical	urgency.
PRINCIPLE	03
Zero	Extra	Clicks	—	The	Piggyback	Rule
Every	AI	action	either	
replaces
	an	existing	click	(auto-fill)	or	
piggybacks
	on	one	the	doctor	already	makes
(suggestions	on	section	focus).	Net	new	interactions	must	be	≤	1.
PSYCHOLOGY
Preattentive	processing
	—	the	brain	processes	color,	size,	and	position	before	conscious	reading.	Red	=
danger.	Green	=	normal.	Arrows	=	trends.	The	doctor	"knows"	the	status	before	finishing	the	label.
PSYCHOLOGY
Information	Foraging	Theory
	—	doctors	follow	"scent"	like	predators.	Highest-value	information	at	the	top.
When	scent	drops,	they	abandon.	The	summary	hierarchy	must	maintain	scent	from	top	to	bottom.

PRINCIPLE	04
Safety	Is	Non-Negotiable,	Non-Dismissible
98.2%	allergy	documentation	gap	=	patient	risk.	Allergy	banners:	always	visible,	always	red,	always	first,	never
dismissible.	Drug-drug	interaction	checking	is	identified	as	a	critical	gap	(100%	unchecked	today)	and	is	earmarked	for
a	future	release	—	v0	focuses	on	allergy-based	safety	as	the	highest-impact,	lowest-complexity	intervention.

(70%	of	dictations).	If	a	summary	reads	like	a	discharge	note,	it's	too	long.
